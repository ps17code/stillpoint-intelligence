# Major Companies — GeO₂ / GeCl₄ Component Layer

## Teaser
Six companies convert raw germanium into the fiber that carries the world's data — and the supply chain's fate depends on their capacity decisions

## Title
The component layer is defined by one supplier, one dominant consumer, and four manufacturers racing to expand capacity they cannot build fast enough

Major companies

## Umicore — the western GeCl₄ monopoly

**Euronext Brussels: UMI · €3.6B revenue (2025) · €847M adjusted EBITDA**

**What they do at this layer:** Umicore is the sole western producer of ultrapure GeCl₄ at meaningful scale for fiber optic applications. Their Energy & Surface Technologies division (now Specialty Materials) refines raw germanium concentrates, purifies through multi-stage fractional distillation, and produces 6N+ purity GeCl₄ at their integrated Olen facility near Antwerp, Belgium. They also produce GeO₂ powder, germanium wafers for IR optics, and germanium substrates for multi-junction solar cells.

**Financial profile:** 2025 group revenue of €3.6 billion (up 3% YoY), adjusted EBITDA of €847 million (up 11% YoY, 24.0% margin). Share price approximately €16 as of early 2026 — up 62% over the prior year. Germanium-specific segment revenue is not separately disclosed, embedded within the Specialty Materials division. Umicore does not publish GeCl₄ production volumes.

**Germanium relevance:** Constitutes approximately 35% of the world's GeCl₄ market with the highest purity production standards. More than 50% of germanium feed comes from recycled manufacturing scrap secured through long-term tolling agreements. Exclusive offtake partner for DRC (Big Hill) germanium concentrates via Gécamines/STL partnership. EU Commission selected two Umicore germanium projects in February 2026 (process innovation for recovery yields and new recycling technologies), with streamlined permitting and dedicated finance access.

**Strategic position:** Every western fiber preform manufacturer — Corning, Prysmian, Sumitomo, Fujikura — depends on Umicore for GeCl₄. Chinese export controls are structurally beneficial: every tonne China restricts forces western buyers to Umicore, increasing pricing power. Prysmian renewed its long-term germanium supply agreement with Umicore in 2025, committing to 100% recycled/sustainable germanium sourcing. Umicore provides spot purchase, hedging, tolling, and metal leasing services to manage customer price volatility.

## Corning — largest GeCl₄ consumer and fiber preform manufacturer

**NYSE: GLW · $16.4B core revenue (2025) · Market cap ~$110B**

**What they do at this layer:** Corning is the world's largest consumer of GeCl₄ and the largest fiber preform manufacturer. They convert ultrapure GeCl₄ into GeO₂-doped silica preforms using their proprietary OVD (Outside Vapor Deposition) process, then draw those preforms into optical fiber. Corning is vertically integrated from preform through fiber to finished cable.

**Financial profile:** 2025 core revenue of $16.4 billion (13% YoY growth). Q3 2025 optical communications revenue of $1.65 billion (+33% YoY). Full-year 2025 optical communications revenue of $6.27 billion (+35% YoY). Optical segment net income grew 69% YoY in Q3 2025. Market cap grew from $40.7B (January 2025) to approximately $110–114B (March 2026) — a 168% appreciation. Stock reached all-time high of $160.13 on February 25, 2026.

**Germanium relevance:** As the world's largest OVD preform manufacturer, Corning is almost certainly the single largest consumer of 6N+ GeCl₄ globally. Enterprise network optical sales grew 58% YoY (Q3 2025) driven by Gen AI products. Meta's $6 billion multi-year agreement (announced January 2026) for optical fiber and cable through 2030 equals Corning's entire 2025 optical communications revenue from a single customer. Corning's Springboard plan was upgraded to $11 billion in investment through 2028, including a $170–268M Hickory, North Carolina expansion that will become the world's largest fiber-optic cable plant.

**Strategic position:** Corning's quarterly optical communications revenue is the highest-quality public leading indicator for AI infrastructure fiber consumption — it ships before the racks are installed. Running at 100% capacity with backlogs extending into 2027. Every preform expansion Corning undertakes increases GeCl₄ demand from Umicore, coupling their growth trajectory to Umicore's output capacity.

## Prysmian — largest cable manufacturer, recently integrated into preforms

**Borsa Italiana: PRY · €17.0B revenue (2024) · Market cap ~€31B**

**What they do at this layer:** Prysmian is the world's largest fiber cable manufacturer by volume — 30M+ km/yr across 27 plants — and recently expanded upstream into preform manufacturing through its 2024 North American preform capacity acquisition. This makes Prysmian a vertically integrated preform-to-cable producer, reducing dependency on external preform supply.

**Financial profile:** 2024 group revenue of €17.0 billion (0.5% organic growth). Adjusted EBITDA of €1.93 billion (up 18.4% YoY). Net profit of €748 million (vs. €547M in 2023). Diluted EPS of €2.52. Completed acquisition of Encore Wire in July 2024 for €3.9 billion — the largest acquisition in company history. Market cap approximately €31 billion as of March 2026.

**Germanium relevance:** Holds over 18% global fiber market share. 2024 North American preform acquisition provides domestic preform capacity with all glass sourced from US origin (BABA compliant). Renewed long-term germanium supply agreement with Umicore in 2025, locking in sustainable sourcing but codifying single-supplier dependency. Operating at full capacity with no relief before late 2027. Invested in Relativity Networks for hollow-core fiber production at Eindhoven facility — a strategic hedge against germanium dependency.

**Strategic position:** Prysmian's dual strategy — locking in Umicore GeCl₄ supply while simultaneously investing in HCF alternatives — positions it for both the germanium-constrained present and the potentially germanium-free future. Build America Buy America compliance for North American preform operations is a competitive advantage as BEAD funding flows into construction.

## YOFC — China's largest fiber manufacturer with domestic GeCl₄ access

**HKEX: 6869 · China's #1 fiber producer**

**What they do at this layer:** YOFC (Yangtze Optical Fibre and Cable) is China's largest and the world's largest fiber preform manufacturer by capacity (3,500 tonnes per annum). They produce preforms using all three major methods — PCVD, VAD, and OVD — making them the only company to independently develop and mass-produce preforms via all three technologies. YOFC also operates the most advanced hollow-core fiber program in China.

**Financial profile:** Hong Kong-listed (6869.HK). April 2025 placing of 28.2 million shares at HK$12.40/share. Nomura initially downgraded 2024–2025 revenue forecast by 19% and profit by 25–35% on weaker-than-expected telecom fiber demand, then upgraded to "Buy" with HK$15 target price due to AI network product potential.

**Germanium relevance:** YOFC sources GeCl₄ from domestic Chinese suppliers — Yunnan Chihong, Nanjing Germanium, GRINM — completely insulated from MOFCOM export controls that constrain western competitors. This creates a permanent structural cost and availability advantage. Chinese manufacturers operate in a parallel supply chain immune to the constraints affecting Corning and Prysmian.

**Strategic position:** Joint venture with Shin-Etsu (Shin-Etsu YOFC Hubei) for preform production. Leading hollow-core fiber developer: achieved world-record 0.040 dB/km attenuation, 91.2 km single preform drawing length, and demonstrated 32×1.2 Tb/s transmission with China Unicom. Launched "INFINITE-2030 Plan" for 50+ global HCF commercial deployments. **YOFC is simultaneously the largest beneficiary of the current germanium-doped fiber supply chain and the company most aggressively developing the technology that could eventually replace it.**

## Shin-Etsu Chemical — diversified Japanese manufacturer with China JV access

**TYO: 4063 · ¥2.56T revenue (FY2025) · Market cap ~$73B**

**What they do at this layer:** Shin-Etsu manufactures optical fiber preforms across three facilities: Kashima Plant (Japan), Shin-Etsu Jiangsu Optical Preform (China), and Shin-Etsu YOFC Hubei (China joint venture). The China operations provide dual supply access — Japanese production for the western market, Chinese production insulated from export controls.

**Financial profile:** FY2025 revenue of ¥2.56 trillion (up 6.06% YoY). Market cap approximately $73.3 billion. The optical fiber segment is a small fraction of total revenue (Shin-Etsu's primary businesses are PVC/chlor-alkali, semiconductor silicon wafers, and electronic materials). Specific optical fiber segment revenue is not separately disclosed.

**Germanium relevance:** Announced ¥18 billion capital investment for global optical fiber preform capacity expansion. The Shin-Etsu YOFC Hubei joint venture provides access to Chinese domestic GeCl₄ supply while maintaining Japanese manufacturing for western customers dependent on Umicore.

**Strategic position:** Geographic diversification reduces single-source GeCl₄ vulnerability. The China JV structure provides optionality that pure western manufacturers lack — Shin-Etsu can shift production between Japanese (Umicore-sourced) and Chinese (domestically-sourced) facilities depending on export control conditions.

## Sumitomo Electric — innovation-focused preform and fiber manufacturer

**TYO: 5802 · ¥4.68T revenue (FY2024) · Record results**

**What they do at this layer:** Sumitomo Electric manufactures optical fiber preforms and draws fiber across the full value chain. The company is particularly focused on next-generation fiber technology, having developed ultra-low-loss multi-core fiber (MCF) with 0.1397 dB/km loss — the lowest attenuation ever achieved for multi-core architectures.

**Financial profile:** FY2024 net sales of ¥4.68 trillion (6% YoY increase) — a record high. Operating profit of ¥320.7 billion. Achieved FY25 financial targets one year ahead of schedule. Infocommunications segment benefited from strong datacenter product demand.

**Germanium relevance:** Significant GeCl₄ consumer for preform production. Multi-core fiber development (shortlisted for Industry Awards 2024 at ECOC) could change the germanium consumption equation: MCF increases fiber-km per route-km of cable, potentially reducing total preform demand per unit of network capacity — though this effect is marginal in the near term.

**Strategic position:** Innovation-focused with proprietary low-loss fiber technology that commands premium pricing. Less exposed to commodity fiber capacity constraints than top-3 manufacturers due to specialization. Vertically integrated from preform to cable with growing datacenter connectivity demand driving segment growth.
