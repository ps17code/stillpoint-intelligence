# Catalysts — GeO₂ / GeCl₄ Component Layer

## Teaser
The China ban expiry, $600B in hyperscaler capex, and BEAD construction all hit the GeCl₄ supply chain simultaneously in 2026–2027

## Title
Five tightening forces converge on GeCl₄ supply before any easing catalyst can reach market scale

## Category
Catalysts

---

## Overview

The component layer faces a convergence of demand catalysts in 2026–2027 that will stress GeCl₄ supply precisely when the easing catalysts — new capacity, recycling expansion, alternative technologies — are still 18–36 months from meaningful contribution. The imbalance between the timing of tightening forces and the lag of supply responses defines the investment thesis for this layer.

## Tightening catalysts

### 01 — China export ban suspension expires: November 27, 2026

**Impact: Binary event for GeCl₄ supply**

The MOFCOM suspension of the outright US germanium export ban runs until November 27, 2026. After that date, China can reimpose the full ban with a single announcement — no legislative process, no warning period. The broader global licensing requirement (August 2023) remains in force regardless.

For GeCl₄ specifically: even during the suspension period, every shipment requires individual MOFCOM license approval. The licensing regime introduces 4–8 week approval cycles that prevent reliable supply planning for preform manufacturers operating on 18–24 month production horizons.

**Markets will begin pricing this risk 3–6 months in advance.** The May–August 2026 window is when germanium and fiber pricing will reflect the probability-weighted impact of ban reimposition.

### 02 — Hyperscaler AI capex drives GeCl₄ demand to structural limits

**Impact: 36x fiber multiplier per AI datacenter rack**

AI-focused datacenters require approximately 36 times more fiber than traditional CPU-based racks. 72-GPU Blackwell nodes require 16x more fiber than traditional cloud switch racks. Global data center fiber demand surged 75.9% year-on-year in 2025 and is projected to account for 30% of total global fiber demand by 2027 (up from 5% in 2024).

Five hyperscalers will spend $660–690 billion on infrastructure in 2026: Amazon at $200B, Google at $175–185B, Microsoft at ~$120B, Meta at $115–135B, Oracle at $50B. Roughly 75% targets AI infrastructure. Every dollar of AI infrastructure spend generates downstream fiber demand. Every fiber-km of demand requires GeCl₄. The capex number propagates backwards through the supply chain: **more datacenters → more fiber cable → more fiber strand → more preforms → more GeCl₄ → more germanium.**

Microsoft alone has deployed 120,000 miles of dedicated fiber for its AI Wide Area Network. Nearly 40 new submarine cable systems are entering service in 2026 (up from 15 in 2025). The US needs to add 213.3 million fiber miles by 2029, doubling from the current 159.6 million.

### 03 — BEAD program fiber deployment begins competing for preform capacity

**Impact: $42.45B federal broadband pulling from same GeCl₄ pool**

53 states and territories have received NTIA approval; 38 have signed award agreements as of March 2026. First groundbreaking expected in early 2026 with peak construction running 2026–2030.

BEAD deployments will drive sustained demand for standard G.652.D fiber throughout 2026–2027, competing directly with AI infrastructure for limited preform capacity. The US government is simultaneously trying to connect rural homes and build AI infrastructure — **from the same supply chain that is already sold out through 2026.**

### 04 — New preform capacity commitments increase GeCl₄ demand

**Impact: Every new preform line is a new GeCl₄ consumer**

Corning's $170–267.9 million Hickory, North Carolina expansion (announced October 2025) will create the world's largest fiber-optic cable plant. Corning's Springboard plan was upgraded to $11 billion in investment through 2028. Shin-Etsu announced an ¥18 billion capital investment for global optical fiber preform capacity expansion.

Each new preform line commissioned increases demand for GeCl₄. The expansion intended to relieve the fiber shortage simultaneously tightens the GeCl₄ shortage. **Capacity expansion at the preform layer is demand expansion at the GeCl₄ layer.**

### 05 — Defense stockpiling and fiber-guided munitions

**Impact: New demand category consuming non-recoverable fiber**

The US DoD has invested $540M+ in critical mineral projects. Executive Order 14241 authorizes DoD to directly facilitate private capital into domestic mineral production. DPA Title III funds have already gone to germanium production at 5N Plus ($18.1M awarded December 2025).

Fiber-guided drone munitions consume 10–40 km of single-mode fiber per flight — disposable, non-recoverable. Russia's procurement alone jumped from <1% to 10.5% of global fiber demand in 2025. Military orders receive priority access to the highest-purity preforms, creating commercial shortages.

US-Congo mineral deals (January 2026) position DRC germanium to supply US strategic reserves. Defense demand is price-insensitive and structurally persistent — it does not respond to market signals the way commercial demand does.

## Easing catalysts

### 06 — Umicore EU-backed capacity expansion

**Timeline: 2026–2028 · Incremental, not transformative**

The European Commission selected two Umicore germanium projects in February 2026: (a) process innovation to increase germanium recovery yields, and (b) new recycling technologies for complex waste streams. EU support includes streamlined permitting and dedicated finance access.

Umicore anticipates sustained top-line momentum in Specialty Materials in 2026, underpinned by good demand for germanium products. However, these are process improvement projects, not new greenfield capacity. Incremental yield gains of 5–15% are meaningful but do not resolve the structural supply-demand gap.

### 07 — 5N Plus DoD-funded expansion

**Timeline: Meaningful output 2027–2029 · Up to 20t/yr at full scale**

DoD awarded 5N Plus $18.1 million in DPA Title III funds (December 15, 2025) for St. George, Utah facility expansion. Target: up to 20 metric tonnes of high-purity germanium per year from industrial residues and mining byproducts over 48 months. Very little revenue impact in 2026; benefits beginning in 2027 but more meaningful in 2028–2029 due to installation and ramp timelines. Also expanding solar cell production capacity by 25% in 2026 via AZUR SPACE subsidiary.

### 08 — DRC germanium reaching conversion stage

**Timeline: 2025–2028 · Concentrates flowing to Umicore for refining**

Gécamines subsidiary STL began exporting germanium concentrates from Lubumbashi in October 2024 to Umicore for processing. DRC's hydrometallurgical plant (commissioned October 2023) should enable DRC to supply up to 30% of world's germanium demand at full scale. But conversion to GeCl₄ happens at Umicore in Belgium — DRC adds raw feed but does not add GeCl₄ conversion capacity. **The DRC story eases the germanium metal bottleneck but reinforces the Umicore component-layer bottleneck.**

### 09 — Hollow-core fiber commercialization

**Timeline: Niche deployments 2026 · Meaningful market share 2028–2030**

Microsoft achieved 1,280 km deployed HCF with zero field failures by early 2026 (0.091 dB/km loss). Target: 15,000 km by late 2026, over 75% of global HCF installations. Manufacturing partnerships with Corning (North Carolina) and Heraeus (Europe/US). Relativity Networks–Prysmian partnership producing HCF at Prysmian's Eindhoven facility.

Hollow-core fiber uses air — not germanium-doped glass — as the transmission medium. It eliminates germanium from the fiber layer entirely. But near-term impact is limited: HCF deployments in 2026–2027 remain niche (~20,000 km total vs. billions km global installed base). Meaningful market share (>10% of new datacenter interconnect fiber) is achievable only by 2028–2030.

### 10 — Non-Chinese recycling and refining capacity

**Timeline: 2026–2027 · Combined ~55t/yr vs. 170t needed to replace China**

Germany's Stade refinery plans to restart by 2027, adding 40 tonnes/year. Kazakhstan's Padvolar refinery targets 15 tonnes/year from H2 2026. Combined with existing western recyclers, this adds meaningful but insufficient volume to close the supply gap.

## Takeaway

The tightening catalysts hit in 2026. The easing catalysts arrive in 2027–2029. **The gap between demand acceleration and supply response is 12–24 months — and it is during this window that GeCl₄ availability will determine which fiber manufacturers can produce and which cannot.**
