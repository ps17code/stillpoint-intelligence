## Data Confidence Summary

**HIGH confidence (>70% of claims):** Corporate financial data from audited filings, market cap data, government program allocations, regulatory records, demonstrated technology specifications, and well-documented transactions.

**MEDIUM confidence (20% of claims):** Market size projections, labor shortage estimates, aggregate capex totals (sum of individual company guidances with some estimation), market share ranges, and construction cost estimates.

**LOW-MEDIUM confidence (<10% of claims):** Long-range market forecasts (8+ years), nuclear SMR deployment timelines, research-stage technology readiness dates (MOSAIC), and single-survey results extrapolated to the industry.

## Key Data Gaps

1. **Turner Construction detailed datacenter financials:** Turner does not break out datacenter revenue or backlog separately — the 37% estimate is derived from project tracking databases.
2. **NVIDIA allocation model specifics:** The methodology NVIDIA uses to allocate GPUs among hyperscaler customers is commercially confidential.
3. **Nuclear PPA pricing:** The Microsoft-Constellation TMI PPA price ($100–110/MWh estimate) is not publicly confirmed by either party.
4. **Liquid cooling vendor market share:** Vertiv, CoolIT, and Asetek do not disclose datacenter liquid cooling revenue separately from total thermal management.
5. **Actual grid interconnection completion rates:** The 15–20% completion rate for projects in the interconnection queue is an industry estimate, not a tracked metric.
